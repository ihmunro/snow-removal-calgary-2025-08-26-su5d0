Winter Safety Tips: Preventing Slips and Falls on Icy Calgary Surfaces

Calgary's winter weather can create hazardous conditions for pedestrians and property owners alike. Understanding how to prevent slips and falls is crucial for maintaining safety during the cold months.

Understanding Winter Risks in Calgary

Calgary's unique weather patterns, including freeze-thaw cycles and sudden temperature changes, create particularly challenging conditions for winter safety. The city experiences an average of 128.8 cm of snowfall annually, with temperatures frequently fluctuating around the freezing point, leading to treacherous ice formation.

Essential Prevention Strategies

  • Regular Snow Removal: Clear snow promptly after snowfall to prevent compaction and ice formation
  • De-icing Applications: Use appropriate ice-melting products before and after snowfall
  • Surface Monitoring: Regular inspection of walkways and driveways for ice patches
  • Proper Drainage: Ensure proper water drainage to prevent ice formation

Property Owner Responsibilities

Calgary property owners have specific responsibilities regarding snow and ice removal:

  • Clear sidewalks within 24 hours of snowfall
  • Maintain safe access to properties
  • Ensure proper lighting in high-traffic areas
  • Document maintenance efforts and any incidents

Best Practices for Winter Walking

Pedestrians can reduce their risk of falls by following these guidelines:

  • Wear appropriate winter footwear with good traction
  • Walk slowly and deliberately, keeping your center of gravity over your feet
  • Stay alert for potentially icy areas, especially in shaded spots
  • Use designated walkways and avoid taking shortcuts

Emergency Response Planning

Having an emergency response plan is crucial for property owners and managers:

  • Maintain contact information for emergency snow removal services
  • Keep first aid supplies readily available
  • Post warning signs in hazardous areas
  • Document all incidents and responses
